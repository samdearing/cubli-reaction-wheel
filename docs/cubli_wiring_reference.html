Bring-up order is not optional. 1) Logic on USB only, no battery → I2C bus scan, confirm 0x36 / 0x6B enumerate. 2) IMU first light (raw accel + gyro clean over serial). 3) Madgwick → valid stable quaternion. 4) Only then connect 4S to the driver VM rails, with common ground already tied.

Sheet 1 — Power Architecture & Ground Topology

Motor power (VM, 14.8 V nom) Ground (star return) Logic / PWM (3.3 V) I2C
Three things that will kill the board. (1) Do not power the Teensy from the SimpleFOC Mini's 3.3 V LDO — it sources 10 mA and cannot run the MCU. (2) Do not cut the VUSB↔VIN pad yet — while USB and the 4S coexist on the bench you want them isolated; the cut only happens when the BEC is physically in place. (3) Common ground gets tied at the star node on the distribution board, not opportunistically at whatever GND pin is nearest.

Why three buses: the AS5600's address is hardwired to 0x36 with no address pins, so three of them cannot coexist on one bus. The Teensy 4.1's three native I2C peripherals are exactly why this board was chosen — no multiplexer. Every device board ships its own pull-ups, so one device (or the IMU+encoder pair) per bus is fine; don't add more.

Two ways to cook the board here. (1) The logic-side GND is the reference the DRV8313 compares your PWM against — it rides in the signal bundle, not with the motor return. (2) That 3V3 out pin is a 10 mA LDO; tying it to the Teensy's 3.3 V rail parallels two regulators against each other. Leave it open.

Why these pins and not others. All three phases of a motor must share one FlexPWM module so they share a carrier. On the Teensy 4.1 that leaves almost no freedom: Wire2 eats pins 24/25, which kills FlexPWM1's submodule-2 and submodule-3 X channels, leaving only {0, 1, 7, 8} on that module. FlexPWM3 exposes just one submodule, so it cannot drive a motor at all. FlexPWM4's third submodule only surfaces at pin 2 or 3. The result is forced: PWM2 → M1, PWM4 → M2, PWM1 → M3. Two stray wires is the floor, not a compromise.
Changed from Rev B. The old M3 (8 / 7 / 29) straddled FlexPWM1 and FlexPWM3 — two independent carriers on one motor. That was a real defect, not a cosmetic one. EN moved off 30/31/32 to 3 / 21 / 9 so each motor's five wires (3× PWM + EN + GND) leave the board from one place.
Set the center-align flag. #define SIMPLEFOC_TEENSY4_FORCE_CENTER_ALIGNED_3PWM — SimpleFOC does not center-align or sync 3PWM on Teensy 4 by default. The flag does both, and requires every PWM pin to be FlexPWM (never QuadTimer). All nine pins above satisfy that; pins 10–15, 18, 19 are QuadTimer and must never be used for a phase.
Per-motor EN pins are a bring-up safety feature, not a convenience. Three separate EN lines let you hardware-isolate two motors while you characterize the third. Bring up M1 alone; the other two stay disabled at the driver, not just "commanded to zero" in firmware.

VBAT sense divider — what it is and why it exists

68 kΩ from the 4S bus to a node, 10 kΩ from that node to ground, node → pin 41. Ratio = 10/(68+10) = 0.128. A full pack at 16.8 V lands at 2.15 V — safely inside the Teensy's 0–3.3 V ADC window. Inverted, 3.3 V corresponds to ≈25.7 V of bus, which is the "26 V full-scale."

JobWhy it matters
LiPo undervoltage cutoffKill the motors at ~12 V (3.0 V/cell) before the pack is damaged.
Correct voltage_power_supplyIn voltage-mode torque control the commanded torque is a voltage. If the bus sags and firmware doesn't know, your torque quietly drifts — and so does your tuning.
Phase-3 regen overvoltageTrip the brake chopper before the bus hits the motor's 20 V abs max.
Put a 100 nF cap across the 10 k. The divider's Thévenin impedance is 68k‖10k ≈ 8.7 kΩ, right at the edge of what the Teensy's SAR ADC wants to see. The cap gives the sampling capacitor a local charge reservoir. Quiescent draw is 16.8 V / 78 kΩ ≈ 215 µA — negligible against 650 mAh. This is a spacecraft EPS telemetry channel, verbatim.

Color-band and label all three axis harnesses at both ends. Three identical subsystems is a cross-wiring trap, and a swapped M2/M3 phase bundle presents as a controller sign error you will chase in software for a weekend. Band M1 red / M2 green / M3 blue at both connector ends.
Mounting. Boards on short standoffs, short load path into a structural member, target first mode ≳100 Hz (above the 0–50 Hz wheel excitation band). Strain-relieve the harness at every board. The failure mode here is connector fatigue, not structure — an under-constrained board gives you intermittent I2C that looks exactly like a firmware bug.

Re-connect & verify checklist

What may and may not go on the breadboard. Yes: Teensy, IMU, encoders, I2C, the PWM/EN signal lines, the VBAT divider. That's steps 1–6 and it's exactly what a breadboard is for. No: the VM rail and the motor phases. Breadboard contacts are rated ~1 A against your 6 A peak, contact resistance is tens of milliohms, and the loop inductance turns every PWM edge into ringing. Phases and VM run point-to-point in real wire, with the bulk cap sitting at the driver — not three inches away across a spring contact. Also no: the BEC. USB-only today.
#StepPass criterion
1Battery disconnected, loop key out. Teensy on USB only.Board enumerates; blink sketch runs.
2Continuity check: every GND in the harness → star node. Ohm out each phase bundle end-to-end.<1 Ω to star; no phase-to-phase or phase-to-GND short.
3Wire IMU + AS5600 #1 to Wire (18/19). I2C scan.0x36 and 0x6B both enumerate.
4Scan Wire1 (17/16) and Wire2 (25/24).0x36 on each — one device per bus.
5Stream raw accel + gyro. Rotate the cube by hand about each axis.Axes and signs match the body-frame convention; gyro bias small and stable; noise sane.
6Stream AS5600 angle. Rotate each rotor by hand.Monotonic 0–4095 wrap; no dropouts (dropouts = magnet gap or I2C wiring).
7All EN lines LOW. Now insert the loop key.VBAT sense on pin 41 reads within ~2% of a meter on the pack.
8Enable M1 only. Open-loop voltage_limit ramp, low value first.Smooth rotation, no cogging screech, driver not hot.
9SimpleFOC initFOC() on M1 — sensor alignment.Reports a sane zero-electric-angle and sensor direction; repeats on re-run.
Step 7 is the boundary. Everything before it is recoverable; a wiring error after it is a dead driver, a dead Teensy, or a LiPo event. Do not skip step 2 because "it worked last time" — you are re-mating connectors, which is exactly when a strand bridges.
